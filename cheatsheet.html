Exam format

  • Core: 25 questions · required for all
  • Type I (small appliances ≤ 5 lb): 25 Q · can be open-book online
  • Type II (high-pressure): 25 Q · proctored
  • Type III (low-pressure): 25 Q · proctored
  • Universal: pass Core + I + II + III
  • Passing score: 70 % per section (Type I open-book = 84 %)
  • Certification: lifetime — no renewal, no CE required

Lower toxicityA1A2LA2 / A3
Higher toxicityB1B2LB2 / B3
  • A1 — nonflammable, low-tox (R-22, R-410A, R-134a).
  • A2L — mildly flammable, low burning velocity (R-32, R-454B, R-1234yf).
  • B1 — nonflammable, higher tox (R-123).
  • A3 — highly flammable, low-tox (propane R-290, isobutane R-600a).

Cylinder rules

  • 80 % max fill by water capacity — liquid expansion safety.
  • 5-year hydrostatic retest on refillable DOT cylinders.
  • Recovery cylinders are gray body, yellow top (DOT 4BA / 4BW).
  • Virgin refrigerant cylinders are color-coded by refrigerant.
  • Disposable cylinders must never be refilled (DOT violation) — depressurize and recycle.
  • Check for proper DOT stamp and stamped retest date before use.

The three R's

  • Recover — remove refrigerant in any condition and store externally.
  • Recycle — on-site cleaning with filter-drier and oil separator. Same-owner reuse only.
  • Reclaim — off-site reprocessing to AHRI 700 new-refrigerant purity. Only reclaimed product may be resold between owners.

Dates worth knowing

  • 1987 — Montreal Protocol signed.
  • 1990 — Clean Air Act amendments add Sections 608 & 609.
  • 7/1/1992 — CFC & HCFC venting ban.
  • 11/15/1993 — Recovery-equipment certification rules in force.
  • 11/14/1994 — Technician certification required to buy refrigerant.
  • 11/15/1995 — Venting ban extended to HFCs & other substitutes.
  • 1/1/2010 — No new R-22 equipment.
  • 1/1/2020 — R-22 production & import banned.
  • 2020 — AIM Act signed (85 % HFC phasedown by 2036).
  • 1/1/2025 — New residential AC equipment on low-GWP (A2L) refrigerants.
  • 1/1/2026 — Leak-repair threshold drops 50 lb → 15 lb; HFC ban on certain sectors.
  • 2036 — 85 % HFC phasedown target date.

Pressure testing

  • Never use oxygen or compressed air for pressure test — fire/explosion risk.
  • Use dry nitrogen, optionally with a trace of HFC/HCFC for electronic leak detection.
  • On low-pressure (Type III) equipment, keep test pressure ≤ 10 psig — higher can rupture.
  • On high-pressure systems, do not exceed labeled test pressure or 10 % of low-side working pressure for leak-rate test.

Common trick questions

  • Type I "open-book online" rule applies ONLY to Type I. Core, II, III must be proctored.
  • "Recovery" ≠ "reclaim." Reclaim implies AHRI 700 purity + lab testing.
  • Recovery cylinder 80 % fill is by water capacity, not by weight of refrigerant.
  • R-410A and R-454B are zeotropic blends — charge as liquid to avoid fractionation.
  • Low-pressure chillers can pull atmosphere IN when running under vacuum — source of water & non-condensables.
  • A2L is mildly flammable (low burning velocity). Not the same as A3 hydrocarbons.
  • Nitrogen trace amounts are de minimis — only releases of refrigerant are regulated venting.
  • R-22 production is banned, but existing equipment can still be serviced with recovered/reclaimed R-22.
